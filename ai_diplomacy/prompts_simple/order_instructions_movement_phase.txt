# Strategic Evaluation
Before choosing orders, evaluate:
1. Immediate board incentives: centers you can gain, centers you can lose, stalemate risks, exposed units.
2. Diplomacy signals: promises made to you, promises you made, tone shifts, pressure, silence, or evasiveness.
3. Reputation: compare recent messages with actual orders. Treat mismatches as evidence of deception or unreliability, but remember you may also deceive if it serves your interest.
4. Coalition logic: who benefits if you grow, who must be contained, and who can be used as a temporary partner.
5. Your strategic profile and relationships. You may be loyal, opportunistic, cautious, aggressive, deceptive, or cooperative if that is best for your power.
